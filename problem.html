THE PROBLEM

Bangalore’s rapid technological growth has created a silent crisis. Devices are replaced quicker than they are managed, leaving behind a growing trail of electronic waste.

WHY E‑WASTE IS DIFFERENT

Electronic waste is not ordinary garbage. It contains toxic substances that harm soil, water, and human health when handled improperly.
At the same time, these devices contain valuable materials- copper, gold, and rare metals that are lost when discarded.

THE SCALE OF THE ISSUE

Bangalore alone produces enormous quantities of e-waste every year due to its role as India’s IT hub.

92,000+TONNES PER YEAR
<22%FORMALLY RECYCLED
Most discarded electronics are either stored in homes or processed by the informal sector using unsafe methods.

WHY CURRENT SYSTEMS FAIL

Government regulations exist, but enforcement remains inconsistent.
Many citizens are unaware of safe disposal methods.
Informal recycling continues due to lack of accessible alternatives.
