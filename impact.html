IMPACT & FUTURE

This project obviously does not end with awareness. It demonstrates how structured action, collaboration and accountability can convert e-waste from a liability into an opportunity.

Expected Impact

Reduction in unsafe informal recycling and landfill dumping.
Improved recovery of valuable materials like copper and precious metals.
Greater public awareness and responsible disposal behaviour.
Alignment with national sustainability and circular economy goals.

Limitations

The success of this model depends on consistent public participation.
Enforcement of e-waste regulations remains a challenge.
Logistics and funding are required to sustain large scale operations.

Future Scope

Integration of digital tracking systems for collected e-waste.
Expansion to other urban centres facing similar challenges.
Student led awareness programs and institutional partnerships.

Final Reflection

This project demonstrates that sustainability challenges require interdisciplinary thinking. E-waste management is not only a technological issue, but also a social, economic and governance challenge. Through this inquiry, the importance of systems thinking, collaboration and long term accountability became clear. -Subby, Akshita, Vansh, Rashmi, Abhav.
