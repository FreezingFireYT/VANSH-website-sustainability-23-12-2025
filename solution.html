Inside an E-Waste Recycling Facility

Manual Dismantling

Collected devices are manually dismantled to separate batteries, circuit boards, cables, screens and plastic casings. This step prevents damage to valuable components and isolates hazardous materials at an early stage.

Mechanical Size Reduction

Remaining components are shredded into smaller fragments. This increases surface area and prepares materials for efficient physical separation.

Magnetic & Eddy Current Separation

Ferrous metals are removed using magnetic separation, while non ferrous metals such as aluminium and copper are extracted using eddy current technology.

Material-Specific Extraction

Printed circuit boards undergo advanced recovery processes such as hydrometallurgy or controlled pyrometallurgy to extract precious metals including gold, silver and palladium.

Plastic & Glass Recovery

Plastics are sorted by polymer type and recycled into granules, while glass from screens is treated separately to remove lead and other contaminants.

Hazardous Waste Treatment

Toxic residues such as heavy metals and chemical by products are stabilised and dealt with based on environmental and safety regulations.
